Ephemera are the things nobody meant to save. The matchbook slipped into a coat pocket, the takeout menu folded and forgotten in a kitchen drawer, the coaster that made it home from a bar that no longer exists.

New York City has always been a city of disappearance: storefronts close overnight, institutions that shaped neighborhoods for decades vanish behind plywood and a coming soon sign. The art on their walls, the typography on their menus, the design decisions made by someone who cared deeply about a place most people walked past without looking up. None of it deserved to disappear.

This archive exists not only to mourn the places we have lost, but to make sure what they made does not disappear with them. It is also an invitation: to look more closely at the city while it is still here, to pick up the postcard by the register, to notice the matchbook on the bar. Go looking. Whatever you find, this is a place to bring it back to, and share it with people who care about the same things you do.
